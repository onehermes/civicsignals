CHAPTER 2 — WHO FEELS IT

The people behind the metrics

Behind every statistic, there are real people trying to accomplish something. Meet Maria, James, and Avery—three personas whose daily struggles reveal why the status quo isn't working.

Maria – Working Parent & Citizen

"I just want to quickly see if my family qualifies for benefits."

  • Needs to access information during limited breaks at work
  • Mobile experience is frustrating and confusing
  • Gives up after 3–4 failed attempts to find answers

James – Content Editor

"Updating content feels like untangling a web of HTML."

  • Spends hours making simple content updates
  • Wishes he could focus on content quality instead of markup
  • Frustrated by inconsistent formatting across pages

Avery – Program Director

"We need data-driven insights into how citizens interact with our services."

  • Can't measure whether services are actually helping people
  • Lacks actionable analytics to improve service delivery
  • Struggles to demonstrate impact to stakeholders

CHAPTER 3 — THE JOURNEY

From audit to launch in six weeks

Weeks 1–2

Content and information architecture audit. User research sessions with citizens and content editors to identify pain points and priorities.

Weeks 3–4

UX research and prototyping. Rapid iteration on navigation patterns, content structure, and mobile-first layouts with stakeholder feedback.

Weeks 5–6

Design system and block strategy. Content migration, pattern library development, and final accessibility testing before launch.

CHAPTER 4 — THE SYSTEM

Turning modernization work into repeatable stories

Every modernization project tells a story. CivicSignals helps public sector teams document their journey from problem discovery through measurable impact, making complex transformations understandable and accessible to citizens and stakeholders.

The Story Model

Each modernization project becomes a Story in the system. Stories are organized by Audience (who you're serving) and Theme (the core topic—Accessibility, Performance, Modernization, Governance). Content editors use reusable block patterns to build consistent narratives without code, maintaining design standards automatically while focusing on what matters: telling the story of impact.
